What Omega-3s Really Do (Plain English Explanation:

Omega-3 fatty acids are essential fats. This means your body cannot produce them on its own — you must get them from food or supplements.

There are three major omega-3s that humans need: EPA (Eicosapentaenoic Acid) – for heart function, reduced inflammation, good blood flow.

DHA (Docosahexaenoic Acid) – for the brain, nervous system, memory, focus, and mood.
ALA (Alpha-linolenic Acid) – mostly from plants; the body converts only a tiny amount into EPA/DHA.
EPA and DHA are the powerhouse omega-3s. They directly support:
Healthy cholesterol levels
Reduced inflammation
Smooth joint mobility
Better blood pressure
Heart and artery health
Focus and memory
Eye and brain development
Immune system function
Hormonal balance
Mood regulation
Your body uses EPA and DHA in every cell membrane. They keep cell walls flexible, healthy, and functioning properly.
But the challenge is that most people don’t get enough omega-3s, either because they don’t eat oily fish regularly or because modern diets are dominated by omega-6 fats, which cause imbalance.
This imbalance contributes to chronic inflammation — the root of many modern health concerns.
That’s why omega-3 supplements have become so essential.
