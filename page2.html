What Is Chronic Fatigue?

Chronic fatigue is persistent, long-lasting exhaustion that does not improve with rest. It may last for months or years and often interferes with day-to-day life. Unlike regular tiredness, chronic fatigue:

Does not go away after sleeping
Makes simple activities feel difficult
Impacts concentration and memory
May come with physical weakness, mood changes, or muscle pain

People with chronic conditions such as autoimmune diseases, thyroid imbalance, diabetes, fibromyalgia, heart disease, or chronic inflammation often experience deep fatigue.
